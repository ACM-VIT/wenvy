WENVY

don’t just manage secrets — version them.

A zero-knowledge, SSH-first, end-to-end encrypted platform that syncs your .env the way Git syncs your code. Plaintext never leaves your machine — the server stores only ciphertext it can never read.

see it push → how the crypto works

X25519 · XChaCha20-Poly1305 · Ed25519 · SHA-256 · age

01

The .env file is where security goes to die.

i.

Plaintext sprawl

Keys get pasted into chat, committed by accident, and copied between laptops. Once a secret is in a Slack thread, it is everywhere and nowhere.

leak surface
ii.

Environment drift

Dev isn’t staging, staging isn’t production. Without a single source of truth, “works on my machine” becomes a deploy-time mystery.

dev ≠ staging ≠ prod
iii.

No history, no blame

Who rotated DATABASE_URL last Tuesday — and to what? Flat files have no commits, no signatures, no audit.

zero provenance
iv.

Sharing without revoking

Hand someone a secret and you can never take it back. No rotation, no key hierarchy, no way to cut access when a laptop walks out the door.

irreversible
02

Zero-knowledge by construction.
Not by promise.

Wenvy encrypts every snapshot on your device before it ever touches the wire. Secrets are sealed in a three-layer envelope, so a full database or object-store breach leaks nothing but ciphertext.

Snapshot
Your canonical .env, sealed with a per-repo key using XChaCha20-Poly1305.
Repo key
Wrapped by the team key — rotated on revocation, never re-encrypting old blobs.
Team key
Wrapped for each member’s SSH key via X25519. Add a device, re-wrap a key — that’s it.
Your SSH key
The root of trust. The private half never leaves your machine.

    04
    
Everything a secret deserves.

  


  

    

      01
      
End-to-end encryption

      
Encrypt and decrypt only on the client. The control plane enforces identity and authorization but is cryptographically blind to your plaintext.

      zero-knowledge
    

    

      02
      
SSH-first by design

      
Your existing SSH key is your identity and your decryption root. No new passwords, no browser detours for day-to-day work.

      ed25519 / rsa
    

    

      03
      
Branch-based access

      
dev, staging and production are first-class policy targets. Unmatched branches fall through to default-deny — never an accident.

      exact › prefix › *
    

    

      04
      
Team RBAC

      
Viewer, editor, admin, owner — at org, team and repo scope. Grant a second team read access without cloning a single secret.

      4 roles · multi-team
    

    

      05
      
Three-way merges

      
Concurrent edits diff key-by-key. Genuine conflicts stop and ask — there is no silent last-write-wins for your production database URL.

      no blind overwrite
    

    

      06
      
Rotation & recovery

      
Revoke a member and roll the team key as a checkpointed saga. Lost every device? A BIP39 recovery key brings you back without an admin.

      saga · bip39
    

    

      07
      
CI/CD service accounts

      
Scoped bearer tokens with explicit branch allow-lists and pull-only capability. Pipelines pull production without ever touching governance.

      scoped tokens
    

    

      08
      
Immutable audit

      
Every login, share, rotation and denied write is an append-only event — attributable to a user or a service account, queryable forever.

      append-only
    

  







  

    05
    
Promotion you can actually trust.

  

  
Authorization is evaluated in order — identity, membership, repo role, then branch policy. A write only lands when every gate agrees.
